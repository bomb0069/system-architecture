⚠ RED ZONE = Distributed Monolith (anti-pattern)
Y-Axis — Internal Coupling (Loosely ↔ Tightly Coupled)
Loosely Coupled (top): Modules or services have clear contracts and minimal shared state. A change in one unit does not force changes elsewhere. High cohesion, low coupling.
Tightly Coupled (bottom): Components share databases, call each other directly, or have implicit dependencies. Changing one thing requires changing many others.
X-Axis — Deployment Granularity (Monolith ↔ Distributed)
Monolithic (left): All code ships as a single deployable artifact. One CI/CD pipeline. Simple to reason about, but scales as a unit.
Distributed (right): Services deploy independently. Different teams own different pipelines. Enables independent scaling — but introduces network, latency, and consistency complexity.
⚠ Danger Zone (bottom-right): Distributed but tightly coupled = "Distributed Monolith." Worst of both worlds.
